My thoughts on the whole album#

I’ve been reading this album for about 2 weeks now, and it’s honestly a blast. Each track is so intimate yet powerful and does a great job at guiding you through this ode to the physical and metaphysical world. Maybe God is not a good guy, or maybe we are?#

Both in Dios es un stalker and La porcelana we see a negative point of view on two prominent characters on this album: us, the humans, and the deity in question. For example, in Dios es un stalker the deity is portrayed as an obsessive, controlling person that is proud of having this total power over our lives. On the other hand, La porcelana describes the human as the obsessive one that sacrifices itself towards becoming something more; the track references the story of Ry≈çnen Gens≈ç, a Japanese nun and poet that scarred her own face in order to be accepted in a monastery. Maybe we both suck? Maybe we, as the creation of the deity, are just as obsessive and controlling as the creator? These tracks really stick with me for providing these opposite thoughts in my mind.

The human side of the divine itself#

Probably my favorite track of the whole album, in Mio cristo piange diamanti we get to see this view of Christ as the victim of our own worship towards him; we’re presented with a deity that can shatter the whole world with its presence, and it still can fail at doing so. In the chorus, the presence of crying diamonds might refer to our expectation when worshiping these deities, that our worship has become something to achieve a goal instead of the worship being the goal itself.

Dealing with the Constant Presence of Death#

Ok, this might be the favorite track of the album; it’s megalomaniac and claustrophobic at the same time. For me, it represents the constant presence of death in our lives and how it can cause anxiety day to day.
